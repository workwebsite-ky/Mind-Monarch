Questions Answered

Frequently asked questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

Therapy benefits anyone seeking personal growth, coping strategies, or support. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit. If you're curious about therapy, that curiosity alone is enough to start.

Is everything I share kept confidential?

Absolutely. Everything shared in sessions is strictly confidential and protected by HIPAA regulations. The only exceptions involve imminent safety concerns, which your therapist will clearly explain at the start.

How long does therapy typically take?

It varies by individual and goals. Some clients see meaningful change in 8–12 sessions; others prefer ongoing support for deeper work. Your therapist will regularly review your progress and adjust accordingly.

Do you accept insurance?

We are an out-of-network provider. Many clients receive partial reimbursement through their insurance's out-of-network benefits. We provide detailed superbills to submit to your insurer. A sliding scale is also available.

What is the difference between in-person and virtual therapy?

Research shows virtual therapy is just as effective as in-person for most concerns. Virtual sessions offer flexibility and accessibility. In-person sessions provide a dedicated therapeutic environment. Both options use the same clinical approach.

Can I switch therapists if we're not the right fit?

Absolutely. The therapeutic relationship is crucial to success. If you feel a different therapist might serve you better, we'll arrange a smooth transition at no additional cost. Your wellbeing always comes first.
